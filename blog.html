Be patient, it takes time!

"The day you graduate from childhood to adulthood is the day you take full responsibility for your life." Jim Rohn.

This quote is soo though-provoking. I stopped reading when I came across it and thought about it for a few seconds. I find it soo true.

Once you start taking being reponsible in your life, you get a feeling of maturity. What do I mean by being responsable here? It means taking control over your life. not relying on others anymore, or believing that you have to be responsible even though you haven't yet taken contol over your life.

I'm still relying on my parents for shelter, food and transportation. I don't make money, yet, to have a place, buy food or pay for the bus, but at least I'm aware of it and I'm trying to find a way to do that, it's slow, but there is progress nonetheless. And It feels soo good. it's satisfying to be in control of your life (or trying to).

Another form of being responsible is stop complaining, realizing that whatever happens to you, you are probably reponsible for it. Got a bad grade? Nope, it's not the professor's fault that the exam was challenging, it's your fault not preparing enough, and it may not even be that hard, just see the A's students, why aren't they complaining? I just fucking hate it when we are out of an exam and as soon as you open your month, everyone start complaining. It's like, why the hell would your complain? Nobody forces you to go to school, nobody told you not to prepare for it. Don't blame being a lazy fuck on others. And no, I'm not a great student either, I just hate complaining. (the irony here, i'm complaining).

“Don’t wish it were easier; wish you were better.”

Of course, there are exceptions to that. you are not responsible for your grandma having cancer. There is nothing you can about it but just accept it.

You can be responsible in other mundane ways. Like doing stuf that usually your parents do for you. Like, washing the dishes from time to time, or washing your fucking underwear. Or doing some paper work at some place, don't let your daddy do it for you, go out and do it for youself. and if you are a parent, teach your kidz this early on, I believe it's a good thing.

As all things, it takes time. You can't just have complete control over every aspect of your life over-night. It, obviously, takes years of financial struggle to achieve financial freedom. It takes years of practice to become self-aware and not an emotional vampire. It takes years to find out who you really are, no matter what the fuck that means.

So be patient. don't complain. it's hard. it's boring. it takes time. but it'll be worth it.

See you tomorrow.
